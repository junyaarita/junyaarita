Welcome to Junya Arita's Website

Observational Astronomer (Ph.D. student) at the University of Tokyo

I am a Ph.D. student at the University of Tokyo. My research interests are the galaxy formation, super massive black holes, and the coevolution of galaxies and black holes. Especially, I study high-$z$ ($z\gtrsim6$) quasars with large telescopes (e.g., Subaru, JWST) and archival data.

Research

I am involved in "Subaru High-$z$ Exploration of Low-Luminosity Quasars" (SHELLQs). SHELLQs have discovered many low-luminosity quasars using Hyper Suprime-Cam Subaru Strategic Program (HSC-SSP) data and identified them spectroscopically. Please visit SHELLQs website, which summarizes the SHELLQs papers and data.

My research focuses on the large-scale environments of the low-luminosity quasars. Understanding where quasars are formed at high-$z$ will give us a hint about the origin of the coevolution. Currently, I am investigating the low-luminosity quasar environment by detecting the sorrounding galaxies with Subaru and JWST. Please check our JWST program (Go cycle 4, ID: 7519, PI: Junya Arita).
